The POS, re-imagined

Customer Engagement without the Middleman

Customer expectations have changed, and we want to use technology to connect with everyone. Big corporations can meet these expectations because they can afford complex technology. A small business cannot. The only technology that a small businesses can afford, the point-of-sale, has remained essentially unchanged, and is rarely involved in any customer engagement. This gap between today's customer expectation and the primitive small business POS is being filled by a slew of middleman apps and sites.

The popularity of these middleman apps validates the changing behavior of the customer, but they impose a penalty on the small business, for being small. The third parties charge exhorbitant fees, steal loyalty to themselves and, in the worst-case, turn the mom-and-pop business into the serfdom of 'Dark Kitchens'.

Can a small business bypass the middleman and satisfy customer expections on its own? The obvious choice would be 'upgrade' the POS, bring it into the modern era of customer engagement. But this is not an easy task. The technologies must be modified and adapted. The resulting system must still be super easy to use, so that the mom-and-pop owners do not need to hire an IT manager.

We took on this challenge, and the result is Chalkboards. Chalkboards re-imagines the Point of Sale evolved for the future, fluent in all the latest technologies. Whereever possible, Chalkboards eliminates the middleman, and serves as a Point of Engagement. We envision a world where small businesses benefit from technology instead of paying a penalty for being small, and still retain their individuality and warmth.
