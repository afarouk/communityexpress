The POS Re-Imagined

The Chalkboards story

IBM's Electronic Cash Register turned the cash register into a computer. That was just the beginning of a revolution that has seen computers become ubiquitous in our everyday lives. Today, large corporations, with huge IT budgets, take full advantage of all the new technologies to engage with their customers, advertise to them, increase their profits. But the small business POS has remained essentially unchanged.

This gap between today's customer expectation and the primitive small business POS is being filled by a slew of third-parties. The popularity of these apps and sites with customers hides the pain they cause to the small business. The third parties charge exhorbitant fees, encourage loyalty to themselves, and turn the mom-and-pop business into a form of serfdom.

Bringing the old POS into the modern era of customer engagement is no easy task. The numerous technologies must be integrated from the inside out. But the resulting system must still be super easy to use, so that the mom-and-pop owners do not need to hire an IT manager.

We took on this challenge, and the result is Chalkboards. The technologies are molded, adapted and scaled to fit the small business needs and abilities. For instance, lets take the task of identifying your most promising customers. A corporation, lacking any personal contact with you, must rely on all sorts of metrics to decide who is promising. Such metrics are not available for a small business owner. But he has something the corporation doesn't - real face-time, and he can decide instinctively who he is promising without any need for metrics. The rest of the technology of enticing such a promising customer could be the same (coupons, discounts...), but the decision making process can be quite different.

Chalkboards re-imagines the Point of Sale evolved for the future, fluent in all the latest technologies, as a Point of Engagement.
